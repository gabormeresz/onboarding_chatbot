# Equipment and Hardware Policy

This policy defines the standards, eligibility, and procedures related to company-provided equipment. The goal is to ensure employees have reliable tools to perform their duties while maintaining cost efficiency, security, and accountability.

---

## Standard Equipment

Each employee is entitled to the following **standard equipment** upon onboarding or role change:

- 1 company-issued laptop (role-appropriate specification)
- 1 external monitor (minimum 24")
- Keyboard and mouse
- Laptop charger and necessary cables
- Pre-installed operating system and security software

## Role-Based Equipment Guidelines

### Software Engineer

- Laptop with minimum 16 GB RAM
- External monitor (24")
- Keyboard and mouse

### Data Scientist

- Laptop with minimum 32 GB RAM
- High-performance CPU / optional GPU
- External monitor (27" recommended)

### Manager

- Laptop with minimum 16 GB RAM
- External monitor (24")
- Webcam and headset for meetings

---

## Additional Equipment

Additional or upgraded equipment can be requested if:

- The role or project explicitly requires it
- There is a clear business justification
- The request is approved by the direct manager

**Examples of additional equipment:**

- Second external monitor
- Docking station
- Headset (noise-cancelling)
- External keyboard or ergonomic mouse
- Webcam
- Standing desk accessories (office-only, where applicable)

---

## Equipment Request Process

- All equipment requests must be submitted via the **IT Support Portal**.
- The request must include:

  - Equipment type and quantity
  - Business justification
  - Office or home delivery location
  - Required delivery date (if time-sensitive)

- Manager approval is required for:
  - Non-standard equipment
  - Replacement outside warranty
  - Upgraded specifications

---

## Equipment Replacement & Repair

- Faulty or damaged equipment must be reported immediately via the IT Support Portal.
- IT Support will assess whether:

  - Repair is possible, or
  - Replacement is required

- Temporary replacement equipment may be provided during repair, subject to availability.

## Security & Usage Rules

- Company equipment must be used primarily for work-related activities.
- Installation of unauthorized software is prohibited.
- All devices must:

  - Use full-disk encryption
  - Have up-to-date security patches
  - Be protected by strong authentication (password, biometrics)

- Lost or stolen equipment must be reported immediately to:
  - IT Support
  - IT Security

---

## Remote Work & Home Office Equipment

- Company equipment remains company property.
- Employees are responsible for:

  - Safe storage
  - Proper usage
  - Returning equipment upon request or offboarding

- Home office equipment may be subject to local labor and tax regulations.

---

## Equipment Return & Offboarding

- All company-issued equipment must be returned:

  - On the last working day, or
  - As instructed by IT or HR

- Failure to return equipment may result in:
  - Access suspension
  - Financial liability, where permitted by law
