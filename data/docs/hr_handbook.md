# HR Handbook – Company Internal

This handbook outlines the core internal HR policies applicable to all employees. It complements individual employment contracts and local labor regulations. In case of conflict, local laws and contractual terms take precedence.

---

## 1. Working Hours and Home Office Policy

Employees are expected to work **40 hours per week**, typically Monday to Friday.

### Core Working Hours

- Core working hours are **10:00 – 15:00 CET**
- Employees must be reachable during core hours unless on approved leave or sick leave
- Working hours outside core hours may be flexible, subject to role and team agreements

---

### Home Office Policy

- Employees may work remotely up to **2 days per week**
- Starting from **September 1, 2025**, the home office allowance increases to **3 days per week**
- Home office days do **not require manager approval** if they remain within the allowed limit
- Home office arrangements must not negatively impact:
  - Team collaboration
  - Client availability
  - Business operations

**Exceptions**

- Requests exceeding the allowed number of home office days require **HR approval**
- Full remote arrangements are evaluated on a case-by-case basis

---

## 2. Vacation and Time Off

### Annual Leave

- Annual paid leave entitlement: **25 days per calendar year**
- Vacation days are accrued according to local labor law
- Vacation requests must be submitted and approved in the HR system in advance

### Sick Leave

- Sick leave must be reported via the HR system **before 10:00 CET** on the first day of absence
- Employees must inform their direct manager as soon as possible
- Medical certificates are required for sick leave exceeding **3 consecutive working days**

### Other Time Off

- Public holidays are observed according to local legislation
- Special leave (e.g. parental, bereavement) is handled according to legal and company policy

---

## 3. Benefits and Compensation

### Compensation

- Salary is defined in the employment contract and paid monthly
- Salary reviews are conducted periodically, based on performance and company policy

### Benefits

- Cafeteria budget: **500 EUR per year**
- Private health insurance is provided **after successful completion of the probation period**
- Additional benefits may be offered based on role, location, or tenure

### Probation Period

- Probation period duration: **6 months**
- During probation, performance and cultural fit are evaluated
- Shorter notice periods apply during probation, as defined in the employment contract

---

## 4. Internal Communication

### Official Communication Channels

The following channels are considered official for internal communication:

- Corporate Email
- Microsoft Teams

Employees are expected to regularly check these channels during working hours.

### HR Communication

- HR-related questions, requests, and documentation must be submitted via the **HR ticketing system**
- HR does not process official requests via private email, chat, or verbal communication

---

## 5. Compliance and Conduct

- Employees must comply with company policies, internal procedures, and applicable laws
- Confidential company information must not be disclosed to unauthorized parties
- Violations of internal policies may result in disciplinary action

---

## 6. Policy Updates

- The company reserves the right to update this handbook as needed
- Employees will be notified of significant changes via official communication channels
