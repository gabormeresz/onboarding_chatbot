# HR FAQ – Frequently Asked Questions

This document provides answers to common HR-related questions. For topics not covered here, employees should contact HR through the official HR system or ticketing channel.

---

## Home Office Questions

**Q: Can I work fully remote?**  
A: Full remote work is not supported by default. Exceptions may be granted with HR and manager approval and are evaluated on a case-by-case basis, taking into account role, team needs, and legal requirements.

**Q: Can I change my home office days every week?**  
A: Yes, home office days can be adjusted weekly as long as:

- The company’s home office policy is respected
- Team coordination is ensured
- The direct manager is informed

**Q: Is there a limit on home office days?**  
A: Yes, the maximum number of home office days is defined by company policy and local regulations. Please refer to the official Home Office Policy for details.

**Q: Do I need to be available during specific hours while working from home?**  
A: Yes, employees are expected to be available during core working hours unless otherwise agreed with their manager.

---

## Probation Period

**Q: What happens during the probation period?**  
A: During the first 6 months of employment, performance, collaboration, and cultural fit are evaluated. Either party may terminate employment during this period with a shorter notice period, as defined in the employment contract.

**Q: Can I take vacation during probation?**  
A: Yes, vacation is allowed during the probation period, subject to manager approval and proper workload planning.

**Q: Am I entitled to benefits during probation?**  
A: Yes, standard benefits apply during probation unless explicitly stated otherwise in your employment contract or local policy.

---

## Working Time & Absence

**Q: How do I record my working time?**  
A: Working time must be recorded in the company-approved time tracking system, where applicable.

**Q: What should I do if I am sick?**  
A: In case of illness, notify your manager as soon as possible and record the absence in the HR system according to local regulations.

**Q: Do I need a medical certificate for sick leave?**  
A: Medical certificate requirements depend on local labor laws and company policy. HR will inform you if documentation is required.

---

## Personal Data Changes

**Q: How can I change my address or bank details?**  
A: Personal data changes must be submitted through the official HR system. For security and compliance reasons, HR does not accept personal data changes via email, chat, or verbal communication.

**Q: How long does it take for changes to be processed?**  
A: Most personal data changes are processed within a few working days after submission.

---

## Salary and Compensation

**Q: When is salary paid?**  
A: Salary is paid on the **10th working day of each month**, unless local regulations or bank holidays require a minor adjustment.

**Q: Where can I see my payslip?**  
A: Payslips are available in the HR system and can be downloaded for personal records.

**Q: Who should I contact if my salary is incorrect?**  
A: Salary-related issues should be reported via the HR system or payroll support channel as soon as possible.

---

## Benefits & Allowances

**Q: When do benefits start?**  
A: Benefits typically start from the first day of employment, unless stated otherwise in your contract or benefit policy.

**Q: Can I change my benefits selection?**  
A: Benefit changes are usually possible during defined enrollment periods or after major life events, subject to policy rules.

---

## Contact & Escalation

**Q: How can I contact HR?**  
A: HR can be contacted via:

- The HR system
- The official HR support or ticketing channel

**Q: When should I escalate an HR issue?**  
A: Escalation is appropriate if:

- An issue remains unresolved after reasonable time
- There is a legal, payroll, or compliance concern
